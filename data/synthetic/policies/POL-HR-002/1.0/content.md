# Leave and Absence Policy

**Policy ID:** POL-HR-002  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026  
**Owner:** Human Resources  
**Classification:** Internal  

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines consistent rules for requesting, approving and recording employee leave in the fictional organisation.

## 2. Eligibility

Permanent and fixed-term employees are covered from their first day of employment. Contractors and agency workers follow the leave terms in their individual contracts.

## 3. Annual leave entitlement

Full-time employees receive 25 working days of paid annual leave in each calendar year, in addition to public holidays. Part-time entitlement is calculated pro rata.

## 4. Carry-over

Employees may carry a maximum of five unused annual-leave days into the next calendar year with manager approval. Carried days must be used by 31 March or they expire, unless a statutory exception applies.

## 5. Sickness absence

An employee who is unable to work must notify their manager no later than one hour after their normal start time. A medical certificate is required from the eighth consecutive calendar day of absence.

## 6. Family and emergency leave

Up to three paid working days may be approved in a rolling twelve-month period for an unexpected emergency involving a dependant. Additional time may be taken as annual leave or unpaid leave with approval.

## 7. Request and approval

Planned leave must be submitted in the HR system at least ten working days before it begins. Managers consider team coverage and business commitments and must record approval before travel or other non-refundable arrangements are booked.
