# Remote Working Policy

**Policy ID:** POL-HR-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026  
**Owner:** Human Resources  
**Classification:** Internal  

## 1. Purpose

This policy explains how eligible employees may work remotely while maintaining
client service, collaboration, information security and operational continuity.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to permanent and fixed-term employees of the fictional
organisation.

Contractors, agency workers and external suppliers are not automatically covered.
Their remote-working arrangements must be defined in their contract or statement
of work.

## 3. Eligibility

Employees may request a regular remote-working arrangement after completing
three months of continuous service.

The employee must:

- have completed all mandatory security and data-protection training;
- have no active performance improvement plan;
- be able to perform their role effectively away from an office;
- have a suitable and secure working environment.

Remote working is not an automatic entitlement. Approval depends on the role,
client commitments, operational requirements and team coverage.

## 4. Office attendance

Employees with an approved hybrid arrangement must normally attend their assigned
office at least two days per working week.

A department may require additional office attendance for client meetings,
training, team events or operational activities.

The employee's manager must communicate any temporary change in attendance
requirements with reasonable notice whenever practical.

## 5. Request and approval process

The employee must submit a remote-working request to their line manager.

The request must include:

- the proposed remote-working days;
- the intended remote-working location;
- confirmation that the location meets security and health-and-safety requirements;
- any equipment or accessibility requirements.

The line manager reviews the request with Human Resources where necessary.

Approved arrangements must be reviewed at least once every twelve months or
earlier when the employee changes role, manager, department or primary location.

## 6. Equipment and expenses

The organisation provides a managed laptop and approved security software.

Employees may claim up to GBP 250 once every three years for approved home-office
equipment. Claims require:

- prior manager approval;
- an itemised receipt;
- submission through the corporate expense system within 30 calendar days.

Broadband, electricity, furniture and general household costs are not reimbursed
unless required as a documented workplace adjustment.

## 7. Information security

Employees must use organisation-managed devices for organisational or client work.

Employees must:

- connect through approved secure-access services;
- prevent family members or other individuals from using managed devices;
- lock the device whenever it is unattended;
- avoid discussing confidential matters where conversations may be overheard;
- store documents only in approved organisational systems.

Printing confidential or restricted information at a remote location is not
permitted unless a documented exception has been approved by Information Security.

A suspected loss, disclosure or compromise of information must be reported
immediately through the organisation's security incident process.

## 8. Working outside the home country

Employees must not work from another country without prior written approval from:

- their line manager;
- Human Resources;
- Information Security;
- the relevant Tax or Mobility function.

Approval is required because cross-border working may create legal, regulatory,
tax, immigration, data-residency and client-contract risks.

## 9. Suspension or withdrawal

A remote-working arrangement may be changed, suspended or withdrawn when:

- business or client requirements change;
- security requirements cannot be met;
- performance or attendance concerns arise;
- the arrangement creates an unreasonable operational impact.

The manager must explain the reason and record the decision.

## 10. Exceptions

Exceptions must be documented, time limited and approved by the policy owner and
the relevant control owner.

An exception does not permanently change this policy.

## 11. Policy review

Human Resources reviews this policy at least annually.

The current approved version must be used when assessing a remote-working request.
