# Information Security Policy

**Policy ID:** POL-SEC-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** 1 February 2026  
**Owner:** Information Security  
**Classification:** Confidential  

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes minimum safeguards for the fictional organisation's information, systems and services.

## 2. Information classification

Information must be labelled public, internal, confidential or restricted according to its sensitivity. Restricted information requires the strongest approved access, storage and transmission controls.

## 3. Identity and authentication

Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for remote access, privileged access and access to confidential or restricted information. Credentials must not be shared or reused across personal services.

## 4. Secure handling

Confidential and restricted information must be stored only in approved encrypted services. It must not be sent to personal email, consumer file-sharing services or unapproved collaboration tools.

## 5. Incident reporting

A suspected security incident, lost device or unintended disclosure must be reported to the security service desk immediately and no later than one hour after discovery. Employees must preserve relevant evidence.

## 6. Removable media

Use of removable media is prohibited unless Information Security approves a documented business need. Approved media must be organisation-issued, encrypted and scanned before use.

## 7. Third-party access

Third-party access must have a named sponsor, documented purpose, least-privilege permissions and an expiry date no later than 90 days after activation. Renewal requires a fresh review.
